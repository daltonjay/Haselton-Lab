What we do

Our research areas

We build diagnostics that work where they're needed most — no cold chain, no lab infrastructure, no compromises on accuracy.

01

Adaptive PCR

PCR machines fail in the field because they blindly follow fixed temperature programs. We use mirror-image L-DNA to optically monitor primer annealing and product melting in real time, letting the instrument react to what's actually happening. First described by Adams et al. in 2017, the approach has since been miniaturized to hit a 15-minute PCR benchmark using laser heating.

Molecular Diagnostics
02

L-DNA as a Molecular Mirror

L-DNA is chemically identical to natural DNA but biologically inert — it can shadow PCR reactions without disrupting them. Beyond cycle control, we use L-DNA to validate amplification products within the same sample, build logic circuits that separate true signal from background leakage, and prototype non-enzymatic amplification systems.

Biosensors
03

SNP Detection Without Sequencing

Sequencing is too expensive and infrastructure-heavy for most of the world. We develop oligonucleotide ligation assays and melt-based screens that identify clinically important single-nucleotide variants at the point of care. Targets include HIV drug resistance mutations, antimalarial resistance markers, SARS-CoV-2 variants, and TB rifampicin susceptibility.

Global Health
04

Field-Ready Sample Processing

Extraction is the step that most often breaks in the field. We strip it down to what's necessary — magnetic beads, a transfer pipette, steel wool, and a magnet. Our high-gradient magnetic separation method pulls TB DNA from up to 200 mL of urine or sputum with no commercial kit, no electricity, and no cold chain.

Sample Processing
05

Coffee Ring & Evaporative Diagnostics

When a drop evaporates on a flat surface, particles naturally concentrate at the edge — the coffee ring effect. We exploit this physics to make biomarkers visible to the naked eye without any active instrument. The platform detects the malaria biomarker PfHRP-II and has been extended to TB, with the original design demonstrated on glass slides requiring no electricity or refrigeration.

Microfluidics
06

Point-of-Care Biosensing Platforms

We apply the same low-resource design principles to a range of clinical targets: a microneedle patch that captures Lyme disease spirochetes directly from skin, a cell phone-coupled paper device for ovarian cancer monitoring in urine, smartphone spectroscopy for field-based biosensing, and lateral flow assay enhancement using metal affinity reagents.

Our mission

"Science only matters if it reaches the people who need it most."

At the Haselton Lab, we believe that brilliant engineering must be paired with deep community engagement. Our research begins and ends with a simple question: will this tool actually help someone, somewhere, today?

Rooted in Vanderbilt's tradition of excellence and driven by a passion for global equity, we're building the next generation of diagnostics — affordable, accurate, and accessible.
